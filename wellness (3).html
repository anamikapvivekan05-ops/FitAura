What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda is an ancient Indian system of medicine that focuses on maintaining harmony between the body, mind, and lifestyle.

The term Ayurveda means "science of life", derived from Sanskrit words Ayur (life) and Veda (knowledge).

It explains that every person has a unique body constitution called Dosha, which influences metabolism, energy, and personality.

The Three Doshas

🌬️ Vata

Represents movement and energy. Vata individuals are creative and active but may experience stress and low stamina.

Best lifestyle: calm routines and relaxation.

🔥 Pitta

Represents heat and metabolism. Pitta individuals are focused and strong but may face burnout.

Best lifestyle: moderate workouts and cooling activities.

🌱 Kapha

Represents stability and strength. Kapha individuals are calm but may have low energy levels.

Best lifestyle: active and energetic routines.

    🧘 Yoga & Asanas

    Yoga is one of the world's oldest mind-body practices, originating in ancient India over 5,000 years ago. The word yoga comes from the Sanskrit root yuj, meaning "to unite" — reflecting its goal of harmonizing body, breath, and mind.

    Regular yoga practice builds flexibility, improves posture, calms the nervous system, and strengthens the core. Unlike high-intensity workouts, yoga works from the inside out — releasing tension stored deep in muscles and joints while cultivating mental clarity and emotional balance. It is suitable for all ages and fitness levels, and pairs beautifully with Ayurvedic principles to support your unique dosha type.
    🌿 Reduces Stress & Anxiety
    💪 Builds Core Strength
    🔄 Improves Flexibility
    🫁 Deepens Breath Awareness

    ❤️ Supports Heart Health

    Tadasana

    Mountain Pose · Beginner

    The foundation of all standing poses. Aligns the spine, engages the whole body, and builds a sense of stillness and grounded awareness.

    ⏱ 30–60 sec Posture · Balance

    Balasana

    Child's Pose · Beginner

    A deeply restorative resting pose that gently stretches the hips and lower back while calming the nervous system and relieving stress.

    ⏱ 1–3 min Stress Relief · Rest

    Bhujangasana

    Cobra Pose · Beginner

    Opens the chest, strengthens the spine, and counteracts the forward-hunching posture caused by long hours of sitting at a desk.

    ⏱ 15–30 sec Spine · Flexibility

    Cat-Cow Stretch

    Flow Pose · Beginner

    A gentle flowing movement between two poses that warms up the spine, improves mobility, and synchronises breath with motion.

    ⏱ 2–3 min Spine · Breathing

    🗓 Recommended Weekly Yoga Schedule

    Beginner (15–20 min/day)

    Tadasana · Balasana · Cat-Cow · Savasana
    Focus: posture, breath awareness, gentle flexibility

    Intermediate (30 min · 5 days/week)

    Surya Namaskar · Bhujangasana · Vrikshasana · Savasana
    Focus: strength, balance, spinal health

    Best Time to Practice

    Early morning on an empty stomach is ideal. Evening sessions (2 hrs after eating) help decompress after the day.

    Tips for All Levels

    Never force a pose. Use a mat on a flat surface. Breathe continuously — never hold breath unless instructed.

    "Yoga is not about touching your toes. It is about what you learn on the way down."
    — Practice with patience, consistency, and self-compassion.

    🌅 Dosha-Based Daily Routine

    A consistent daily routine aligned with your dosha is one of the most powerful wellness tools in Ayurveda.

    ☀️ Morning Habits

    • Start with a glass of warm water
    • Gentle yoga — Tadasana, Balasana, Cat-Cow
    • Warm, cooked breakfast with ghee and spices
    • Avoid rushing — give yourself time to ease into the day

    🌙 Evening Habits

    • Light walk or gentle stretching
    • Warm, easily digestible dinner
    • Self-massage with sesame oil before bed
    • No screens 30 mins before sleeping

    ☀️ Morning Habits

    🌙 Evening Habits

    • Balanced, satisfying dinner — never skip meals
